# Initial company profile

This is an initial evidence review, not a deliberate thesis review. The thesis
remains unreviewed. It does not estimate intrinsic value or claim a margin of
safety.

## Executive read

- **Reported fact:** Raffles Medical Group (RMG) is a founder-controlled,
  integrated Asian private-healthcare group spanning primary care, hospitals,
  insurance, wellness, and related services. It reports four hospitals, more
  than 100 multidisciplinary clinics, operations in 14 cities across five Asian
  countries, close to 2,900 employees, and more than 2.8 million patients a
  year. [[vault/companies/SGX/BSL_Raffles-Medical-Group/sources/annual-reports/fy2025.pdf#page=4|FY2025 annual report, PDF p. 4]]
- **Reported fact:** FY2025 revenue rose 1.8% to SGD 765.3 million and PATMI rose
  13.4% to SGD 70.6 million. Hospital Services drove the improvement; Healthcare
  Services revenue and profit declined, while Insurance remained loss-making at
  the segment pretax level. [[vault/companies/SGX/BSL_Raffles-Medical-Group/sources/annual-reports/fy2025.pdf#page=9|FY2025 annual report, PDF p. 9]] [[vault/companies/SGX/BSL_Raffles-Medical-Group/sources/annual-reports/fy2025.pdf#page=215|FY2025 annual report, PDF p. 215]]
- **Calculated metric:** At 2025 year-end, net cash was SGD 261.1 million before
  lease liabilities and SGD 223.4 million after lease liabilities. That is about
  SGD 0.142 and SGD 0.121 per current disclosed share, respectively.
- **Agent inference:** The balance sheet and integrated Singapore franchise give
  RMG resilience, but the evidence does not yet establish high returns on
  incremental capital. Greater China held 30.7% of geographically allocated
  non-current assets but produced only 8.5% of revenue, and the Shanghai and
  Chongqing hospitals continued to make operating losses.
- **Calculated metric:** At the SGD 0.940 close on 2026-07-15, the shares were
  only 4.4% above their 52-week low, but still traded at about 24.7 times FY2025
  diluted EPS and 1.63 times book value. A low position in the trading range is
  not, by itself, evidence of undervaluation.

## Strongest evidence for and against further work

### Supports continued research

- **Reported fact:** A sizeable, integrated Singapore healthcare franchise with
  a long operating history and broad service coverage.
- **Reported fact:** FY2025 hospital profit growth of 15.3%, rising group
  margins, and net cash.
- **Calculated metric:** One-year operating cash flow exceeded PATMI and the
  lease-adjusted cash FCF proxy was SGD 79.9 million.
- **Agent inference:** Owned properties, investment properties, and net cash
  create balance-sheet optionality, provided they can earn adequate returns.

### Contrary evidence and unresolved risks

- **Reported fact:** Shanghai and Chongqing remain loss-making and require
  judgement-heavy Level 3 recoverable-value estimates.
- **Reported fact:** Insurance still reported a SGD 3.1 million segment pretax
  loss. Management said claim frequency and severity remain elevated and did
  not disclose a loss ratio. [[vault/companies/SGX/BSL_Raffles-Medical-Group/sources/announcements/2026-05-13-minutes-2026-agm.pdf#page=9|2026 AGM minutes, PDF p. 9]]
- **Calculated metric:** Greater China holds 30.7% of allocated non-current
  assets but contributes 8.5% of revenue.
- **Reported fact:** A single undisclosed customer accounts for 16.2% of group
  revenue.
- **Reported fact:** The founder controls 56.313%; two children sit on the Board,
  and one is on the Audit & Risk Committee.
- **Calculated metric:** A 24.7x reported P/E and 4.1% earnings yield do not
  provide a visible margin of safety without stronger normalized economics.

## Next evidence needed

1. Build a pre-COVID, COVID, and post-COVID normalized earnings bridge covering
   at least ten years.
2. Separate maintenance from growth capital expenditure and reconcile cash
   capex, right-of-use additions, and lease principal to owner earnings.
3. Obtain China hospital revenue, operating loss, patient volume, occupancy,
   capacity, cash burn, and expected breakeven data by hospital.
4. Obtain Raffles Health Insurance's loss ratio, expense ratio, capital
   requirements, and profitability by product line.
5. Identify the major customer and assess renewal, pricing, and concentration
   risk.
6. Review property valuations and determine which assets are operationally
   essential versus genuinely separable.
7. Evaluate succession, related-party safeguards, and incentive dilution.
8. Review 1H2026 results when released before trading on 2026-07-27.

## Initial conclusion

**Agent inference:** RMG looks like a resilient, cash-generative Singapore
healthcare platform with a strong balance sheet and improving FY2025 hospital
economics. The current evidence does not yet show that China earns an adequate
return, that insurance has reached sustainable profitability, or that the SGD
0.940 price offers conservative value. The correct status is therefore
`candidate`: worthy of deeper normalization and valuation work, with no buy,
sell, position-size, intrinsic-value, or margin-of-safety conclusion.
